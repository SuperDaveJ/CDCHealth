Selecting Policy Options

Comparing Policy Options

After you have assessed each option against the chosen criteria, and only after you have done this, you can compare the policy options.

In some situations, there may be a “clear winner.” For example, one policy option may stand out as being feasible, as having a strong impact on public health, and as being fiscally and economically viable. This may be apparent from the policy option table you complete.

However, in most situations, the prioritization process will be more complicated, and you will have to assess the trade-offs of different options.

The policy analysis table is just one way that you can visually illustrate your analysis so that decision makers can arrive at a decision with a clear and organized understanding of risks and benefits. This can be a useful exercise and once you understand the process, you can adapt it for your needs.
