Prioritizing Policy Options

After you have scored the various options, you will rank them according to the scores you assigned; in other words, you will prioritize them. Prioritization is an important strategy (rather than simply selecting an option) because there may be an opportunity to move forward with more than one option, or to combine options.

For example, a state may choose to address teen drinking and driving by implementing policies for graduated driver's license protocols as well as zero tolerance for those under 21 who are caught drinking and driving.

Just because an option was not at the very top of the list in terms of scoring, does not mean that it should be discarded. This process of prioritizing options (rather than selecting only one) can help you to create a comprehensive policy agenda. During the prioritization process, consider these questions.
