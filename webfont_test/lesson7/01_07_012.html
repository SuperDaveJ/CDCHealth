Effective Communication of Selected Policy Options

CDC Limitations in Communicating Policy Options

Framing Messages for a Wide Audience—Part 2

Framing the message about policy options in a way that will be compelling to decision-makers means:

  • Recognizing what not to say
  • Recognizing how to say it

According to the Right Choices model, “people's health outcomes are determined by their own choices and actions (which, in turn, are determined by their upbringing, values, their knowledge about health, and so on).” With this in mind, it is critical that messages be framed in a way that spurs the intended choices and actions based on the determining factors for the target audience.

Source: Aubrun, A., et al. (2007). External Factors vs. Right Choices: Findings from Cognitive Elicitations and Media Analysis on Health Disparities and Inequities in Louisville, Kentucky. Cultural Logic, Inc.
