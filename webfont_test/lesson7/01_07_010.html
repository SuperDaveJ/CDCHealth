Effective Communication of Selected Policy Options

CDC Limitations in Communicating Policy Options

Highlighting Scientific Evidence and Providing Examples

CDC's role is to provide information on the science and the evidence related to selected policy options so that stakeholders, such as decision-makers, partners, and the general public can make informed decisions. In this role, CDC is permitted to make scientific and policy recommendations and to inform the public and the Congress of Administration positions on legislative and policy-related matters.

Section 503(a) of Division F, Title V, of the FY 12 Consolidated Appropriations Act specifically recognizes executive–legislative relationships and the Executive Branch’s ability to speak directly to policymakers, including working directly with state and local government agencies and Congress.
