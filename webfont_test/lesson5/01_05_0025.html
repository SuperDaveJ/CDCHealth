Assessing Policy Options

Establishing Criteria for Evaluating Policy Options

The trade-offs must be clear to stakeholders and decision-makers, in particular those whose roles are defined by prioritization and resource allocation. For analysis of a policy that impacts public health, consider measuring the option against the three criteria below. This list is not exhaustive. There are plenty of other criteria to select (such as environmental impact, impact on industry or trade, impact on social welfare) which may be important.

  • Impact on public health
  • Feasibility
  • Fiscal and economic impact

You may want to enlist the help of an economist in situations where complex calculations are necessary to determine the fiscal and economic impact.
