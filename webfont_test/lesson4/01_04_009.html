Modifying Existing Policy Options

For example, in some locations, implementing a comprehensive smoke-free air law (covering all workplaces, restaurants, and bars) may not be feasible due to a variety of reasons, including cultural norms, powerful stakeholders, and/or the lack of a window of opportunity for that particular policy. It is incredibly important for tobacco control stakeholders, coalition members, and supporters to sit down with partners early to discuss all potential amendments and exemptions and agree on items that are NOT acceptable. Different jurisdictions are able to adopt and implement laws that differ in strength, based on a number of reasons. As norms change, there may be a shift in what is feasible or publicly acceptable, and a more inclusive policy may be adopted.

In other situations, the policy option may not be a feasible choice for a specific environment due to the inclusion of exemptions or loopholes that will make it more difficult to strengthen the law in the future. For example, if a law includes a provision allowing ventilated smoking rooms, it will become extremely difficult to remove that provision and pass an effective law later.
