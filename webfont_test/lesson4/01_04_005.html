Lesson 4

Identifying Policy Options

Conducting an Environmental Scan

Exploring Available Options

You have defined the public health problem and determined it could potentially have a policy solution. The next step is to thoroughly explore the policy options available. One place to begin is an environmental scan of the print and grey literature.

As mentioned in Lesson 3, sources of information about policy options include the peer-reviewed literature, grey literature, and policy documents from stakeholder groups, professional associations, and non-profits.

You can also consult the span of resources among CDC’s existing policy options. These could be identified through discussions with staff in your organization’s policy office, as well as working with your center, institute, or office Associate Director for Policy to consult with policy analysts and leaders in other CIOs relevant to the problem. Additionally, you can consult with the CDC Office of the Associate Director for Policy in the Office of the Director.
