Identifying Policy Options

Selecting Potential Policy Options

Developing New Policy Options

In addition to looking at trends, it is also important for you to be able to think from a systems perspective. This means understanding how a particular problem fits into the larger system. Thinking from a systemic level enables you to identify which public health problems will be important to address in the future, and it also provides context for the time it might take for a policy to begin to address a trend.

The next important question to answer is whether there is a policy solution to address the underlying cause of the trend. If so, is there a window of opportunity for this policy? Answering this question will help you narrow down your pool of potential policy options.

Listen to Lessons from the Field for an example that illustrates the process of viewing problems from a systemic approach and identifying policy windows of opportunity.
