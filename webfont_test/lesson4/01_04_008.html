Modifying Existing Policy Options

Based on your environmental scan, you may have found policy options already created to address health problems. In such a case, your job is to study each option to determine if it has been effective in the past, and whether it’s likely to be effective in your current situation. Ineffective policies can also inform policy selection, so review those as well.

This is where your understanding of the social, cultural, and political context of the health problem and policy options is crucial. For example, the social context may tell you specific social circumstances that increase vulnerability for transmission of an infectious disease. The cultural context may influence food choices that contribute to obesity. The political context may reflect a lower likelihood that certain policy options often associated with political viewpoints would be explored. Sometimes policies can be scaled back or modified to fit the local social, cultural, and/or political context.
