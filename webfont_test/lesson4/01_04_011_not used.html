Developing New Policy Options

In order to address problems more effectively, you must look for causes and patterns, rather than examining a discrete occurrence. For example, the Georgia Health Policy Center provides education for state legislators about policy options. One of the strategies they employ to stimulate thinking is they encourage the policy makers to answer each of these questions for the health problem at hand:

  • What is the important trend related to the public health problem of interest?Â 
  • What is the shape of this trend over the past several years?
  • Why is this trend happening? (What is the cause? What is responsible?)

It may be helpful to ask yourself these questions as you are gathering information on the public health problem, and formulating ideas about which policy options may be the most appropriate.
