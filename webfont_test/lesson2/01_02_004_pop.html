Ten Principles for Resource Allocation in a Public Health Emergency

The principles emphasized:

  1. The importance of transparency
  2. Outreach and community participation
  3. Balancing individual and communal needs
  4. Avoiding decisions that treat groups differently based on race, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, etc.
  5. Adhering to standard-of-care guidelines
  6. Identifying public health priorities based on scientific evidence
  7. Prioritizing and coordinating initiatives based on core public health functions and essential services
  8. Assessing outcomes of allocation decisions in an iterative process
  9. Documenting the persons responsible for allocation decisions, and
  10. Maintaining the exclusive goal of promoting the health and safety of people when sharing personally identifiable health information

For more details on these ten principles, review the public health report, “Resource Allocation on the Frontlines of Public Health Preparedness and Response: Report of a Summit on Legal and Ethical Issues.”
