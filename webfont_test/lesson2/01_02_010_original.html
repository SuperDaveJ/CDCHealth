CDC’s Role in Policy

Public health professionals like you can play an important role in the policy process by developing partnerships, conducting policy analysis, promoting evidence-based interventions, and communicating findings. CDC strength and expertise lie in the development and analysis of the science to identify, implement and evaluate the health impact of policy and other prevention strategies and tools. When developing communications tools, strategies, and talking points, you should be aware of and comply with the lobbying restrictions that are placed on all federal agencies. CDC employees should also be aware of and educate relevant groups regarding the restrictions on the use of federal funds by grantees and contractors for lobbying. The basis for these restrictions can be found in 18 USC §1913; more about this legislation and the relevant regulations can be found on the CDC Washington Intranet.

Additional or updated restrictions are occasionally added into annual appropriations. Take, for example, this extract from the FY12 Consolidated Appropriations Act. It is particularly important that CDC employees are aware of the restrictions both on the agency and its grantees. More information about lobbying restrictions can be found on the CDC Procurement and Grants Office website.
