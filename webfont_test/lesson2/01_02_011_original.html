Overview of Health Policy

CDC’s Role in Policy

CDC’s role is to deliver evidence-based science to inform decision-making. For example, after analyzing policies that decrease excessive alcohol use and related harms, CDC released a statement saying, "Evidence-based interventions to prevent binge drinking and related harms include increasing alcoholic beverage costs and excise taxes [and] limiting the number of retail alcohol outlets that sell alcoholic beverages in a given area."

Since all federal agencies, including CDC, are subject to statutory restrictions on lobbying, CDC does not lobby for any proposed or pending regulation, administrative action, or order issued by the executive branch of any state or local government or measures pending before a state or local legislature or legislative body. We'll go over these limitations in further detail later in Lesson 7.
