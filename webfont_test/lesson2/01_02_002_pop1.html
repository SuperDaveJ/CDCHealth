Regulation

A federal regulation is one of the basic tools government uses to carry out public policy. Agencies create regulations to implement the authority of laws passed by Congress.

As an example of a regulation, the tobacco industry is prohibited from manufacturing for sale or distribution any tobacco products for which the label, labeling, or advertising contains the descriptors "light," "low," or "mild" (or any similar descriptor). See this website for more information: http://www.cdc.gov/Features/TobaccoControls/
