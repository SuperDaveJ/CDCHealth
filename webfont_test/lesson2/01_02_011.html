Lobbying Restrictions

When developing communications tools, strategies, and talking points, you must comply with the lobbying restrictions placed on all federal agencies. CDC does not lobby for any proposed or pending regulation, administrative action, or order issued by the executive branch of any state or local government, or for measures pending before a state or local legislature or legislative body. We'll go over these limitations in further detail later in Lesson 7.

You should also be aware of restrictions on the use of federal funds by grantees and contractors for lobbying, and educate relevant groups about those restrictions—the basis for which can be found in 18 USC §1913. You can find more about this legislation and the relevant regulations on the CDC Washington Intranet, as well as on the CDC Procurement and Grants Office website.

Occasionally, additional or updated restrictions are added into annual appropriations. For example, click the graphic to read an extract from the FY 12 Consolidated Appropriations Act. You can learn more about how these restrictions affect you by viewing the documents titled “CDC Implementation of Anti-Lobbying Provisions” and “Section 503, Division F, Title V, FY 12 Consolidated Appropriations Act,” included in the lesson resources.
