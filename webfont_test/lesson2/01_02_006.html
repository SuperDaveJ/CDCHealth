Overview of Health Policy

What Is “Policy Analysis?”

As part of the policy development process, you will conduct policy analysis. Policy analysis is a systematic process involving the use of quantitative and qualitative methods to identify, compare, and select policy interventions that will achieve objectives and maximize outcomes most efficiently.

Government and other institutions use policy analysis to develop and implement laws, regulations, procedures, administrative actions, incentives, or voluntary practices and evaluate their impact.

CDC has developed a process to assist you with policy development. Policy analysis is the second step in the process and is a key component of CDC’s Health Policy Competency. Because this course’s focus is on policy analysis, we’ll briefly look at just a subpart of the Health Policy Competency: the Policy Analysis Competency.
