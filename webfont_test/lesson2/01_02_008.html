You are well aware that policy development is rarely a linear process; often the domains of the policy cycle overlap or occur out of order. However, in the ideal scenario, a problem is defined, potential policy solutions are identified, analyzed, and prioritized, and the best solution is adopted and evaluated.

For the purposes of this course, we will focus on the first two domains in the process: problem identification and policy analysis. These steps will be further defined in upcoming lessons.
