Overview of Health Policy

Emerging Public Health Issues and Policy Development

Sometimes policy has to be made before all variables in the equation are certain or known.

For example, The Guide to Community Preventive Services states that there is insufficient evidence to make a recommendation regarding transportation and travel policies as they relate to the potential to increase physical activity, because only one study qualified for review.

However, there may be cases where transportation policy is already being considered by decision-makers, and data regarding the impact on health has been requested.

In such circumstances, when the policy window of opportunity is open, and the issue may call for partial evidence available to inform the decision, one strong study could be considered a promising practice. The findings could be shared with decision-makers with the understanding that they are preliminary.
