What Is Policy?

Resource Allocation and Prioritization

Resource decisions may influence policy options. When resources are scarce, they must be prioritized and allocated judiciously. To assist decision-makers with this challenge, the World Health Organization (WHO), for example, has established ethical principles of resource allocation, including: “efficiency (maximizing population health), fairness (minimizing health differences), and utility (greatest good for the greatest number)” (WHO, 2004).

Resource allocation is essential during public health emergencies. In 2006, 20 academic and practice experts were convened in Washington, D.C., to develop a set of principles for allocation of public health resources during an emergency. A list of ten principles was created.

Decision-makers allocate limited resources based on prioritized policy options selected for implementation. Prioritizing policy options is important because it assists decision-makers in identifying the ones on which they should focus their limited resources.

To read more about the recommendations of the WHO regarding ethical resource allocation, review Guidance on Ethics and Equitable Access to HIV Treatment and Care.
