Overview of Health Policy

CDC’s Role in Policy

CDC’s role in policy is to deliver evidence-based science to inform decision-making. CDC's policy professionals, guided by advanced training in diverse disciplines, provide knowledge of existing health systems, public health methods and approaches, and strategy development. They assess opportunities and risks and analyze issues, trends, programs, and resource allocations. They form strategic partnerships and facilitate policy analysis, formulation, and intervention design and evaluation. They also conduct performance and impact evaluation to determine the effectiveness of public health efforts.

For example, after analyzing policies that decrease excessive alcohol use and related harms, CDC released a statement saying, "Evidence-based interventions to prevent binge drinking and related harms include increasing alcoholic beverage costs and excise taxes [and] limiting the number of retail alcohol outlets that sell alcoholic beverages in a given area."
