Policy as a Tool

Policy has been a useful tool in several of the Ten Great Public Health Achievements in the U.S. from 2001 to 2010, including:

  • Expansion of the number of vaccine-preventable diseases targeted by U.S. immunization policy.
  • Implementation of evidence-based policies by federal, state, and local public health authorities to reduce tobacco.
  • Reduction of birth defects through mandatory folic acid fortification of cereal grain products.
  • Expansion and enactment of seat belt, child safety seat, and graduated driverâ€™s licensing legislation to reduce motor vehicle injuries and fatalities.
  • Expansion and strengthening of laws to prevent childhood lead poisoning.
