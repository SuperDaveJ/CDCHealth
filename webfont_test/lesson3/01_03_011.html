Stakeholdersâ€™ Perspectives on the Problem

Part 2

Remember the problem about teenagers texting while driving that was identified by the policy analyst, and discussed at the beginning of this lesson? Data from the Youth Risk Behavior Surveillance System (YRBSS) tells her the scope of the problem nationwide and provides an understanding of the breakdown by demographic group. However, the survey data alone is not enough for her to develop a complex understanding of the problem. She could interview medical personnel, police officers, school teachers, and counselors in the community to find out more about the circumstances under which teens drink and drive, their attitudes, and other factors that may contribute to the problem.

Engaging stakeholders is a critical part of policy analysis. In this example, the analysts could consult experts in public safety, advocacy groups for injury prevention, or school officials who have talked publicly about the problem.Each stakeholder group may offer insights into what has worked in the past or any challenges to policy implementation that may arise.
