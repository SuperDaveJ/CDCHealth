Define the Problem Which Impacts Public Health

Defining and Scoping the Problem

Part 2

Of course, not every problem statement will neatly fit the “who, what, when, where, and how” frame. Take a look at these two example policy briefs and how the problem was defined therein:

  • Seat Belts
  • Obesity

There are numerous briefs on the CDC and other organization websites that can give you a good idea of how others express problem statements. Check the resources list in this lesson for additional suggestions.

It is important to note that it is not within the scope of a problem definition to point to potential solutions or actions. Likewise, problem definitions should be free of bias and refrain from advocating for a particular viewpoint or course of action.
