Taking all of this information into consideration, you may end up with a problem statement like this one: People getting medical care can catch serious infections called health care-associated infections (HAIs). While most types of HAIs are declining, one – caused by the germ C. difficile (a bacterium that can cause symptoms ranging from diarrhea to life-threatening inflammation of the colon) – remains at historically high levels. C. difficile causes diarrhea linked to 14,000 American deaths each year. Those most at risk are people, especially older adults, who take antibiotics and also get medical care.
